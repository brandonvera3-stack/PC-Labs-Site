Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about PCLabs — from how it works to what it never touches.

Safety & Privacy

Yes, PCLabs is completely safe. It's a read-only diagnostics tool — it never modifies system files, installs background services, or changes your Windows registry without explicit consent. The executable is code-signed, so Windows SmartScreen can verify its authenticity. If your antivirus flags it, that's a false positive common with unsigned or newly released diagnostics software; you can submit the binary to your AV vendor for whitelisting.
PCLabs collects nothing by default. All hardware and software data is read locally and displayed only on your screen — nothing is transmitted to any server. There are no analytics, no telemetry, and no account required. If a future version includes optional crash reporting, it will be opt-in and clearly disclosed. Your PC's information stays on your PC.
Most diagnostics run fine without elevated privileges. However, some hardware sensors — such as CPU voltage, fan RPM readings, and certain S.M.A.R.T. drive details — require administrator access to query via Windows APIs (WMI/IOCTL). PCLabs will prompt for elevation only when needed and will gracefully skip checks it can't access rather than crashing.

How It Works

It does not run in the background, does not install system services, and does not hook into your OS at startup. CPU usage during a scan is minimal (under 2% on most machines) and lasts only a few seconds per check.
The diagnostic scanner is read-only — it identifies issues and explains them in plain language without touching your system. Pro users also get the Game Optimizer, which applies Windows-level settings changes (power plan, HAGS, visual effects, etc.) only when you explicitly click Apply. There's no harm in running it more often, especially after a Windows update, a driver change, or after pushing your hardware with games or creative workloads. Think of it like a quick health check.

Compared to Other Tools

CPU-Z and HWiNFO are excellent tools for raw hardware data — they show you numbers. PCLabs focuses on interpretation and context. Instead of presenting you with 200 sensor readings to decode yourself, PCLabs analyzes your system holistically — checking whether your thermals are actually within safe limits for your specific CPU, whether your RAM is running at its rated speed, whether your GPU is throttling — and surfaces only what matters, in plain English. It's built for people who want answers, not dashboards.
