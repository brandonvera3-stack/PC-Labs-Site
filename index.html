FAQ

Common questions.

Especially about what we support — and what we don't.

Which games are supported for optimization recommendations? ▼
We currently support 42 titles including Cyberpunk 2077, Elden Ring, Call of Duty: Warzone, Forza Horizon 5, Baldur's Gate 3, Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024, Valorant, Apex Legends, Fortnite, and more. The full list is available in the app. We add new games regularly based on user requests and when we have enough hardware profile data to give useful, confident recommendations — not guesses.
Why can't you support every game? ▼
Our game recommendations are based on profiling data from real hardware configurations — not guesswork. To give a confident recommendation for a specific game on a specific GPU class, we need enough data points to trust the numbers. For obscure titles or very new releases, we'd be making things up, which we won't do. If your game isn't supported yet, you can request it in the app and we'll let you know when it's ready.
Will ScanMyPC automatically fix issues or change my settings? ▼
No. ScanMyPC diagnoses and recommends — it does not automatically make changes. We believe you should understand what's being changed and why before anything happens to your PC. Recommendations are displayed with explanations and confidence levels, and you choose what to act on. We do not write to your registry, modify game files, or apply "tweaks" automatically.
How accurate are the FPS estimates? ▼
Estimates are based on hardware profiling data from similar system configurations. We show ranges rather than single numbers specifically because real-world performance varies. Expect ±15–20% variance depending on your exact thermal state, background processes, driver version, and in-game scene. We clearly label these as estimates, not guarantees — that's intentional.
What data does ScanMyPC collect from my PC? ▼
ScanMyPC reads hardware information (CPU, GPU, RAM, storage), Windows system logs (event viewer errors, driver issues), thermal sensor data, and which games are installed via standard Windows APIs. It does not access your personal files, browsing history, or any content. You can review the full data manifest in the app before running a scan. Anonymized hardware profile data may be used to improve benchmark estimates, and you can opt out of this.
My game runs fine — is there still a point in running ScanMyPC? ▼
Possibly, for a few reasons: thermal issues often build up silently before causing problems. A health scan might catch a drive showing early SMART warning signs, or RAM running in the wrong configuration, or a driver conflict you haven't noticed yet. The benchmark is also useful just to understand how your hardware stacks up against the games you want to play in the future. But if everything feels great, the free scan is a good "all clear" checkup and you may not need Pro.
Can ScanMyPC tell me if I should upgrade my hardware? ▼
Sort of. The bottleneck analysis will tell you which component is the likely limiting factor and how much headroom it has. But we deliberately don't push upgrade recommendations — in most cases, there are free or low-cost fixes (drivers, thermal paste, RAM slot configuration, in-game settings) that are worth trying first. If you're genuinely hitting the ceiling of your hardware for a specific game, we'll say so clearly, including what the realistic next-tier upgrade would look like.
Does this work on laptops? ▼
Yes, with caveats. Laptop diagnostics are supported, but power management and thermal behavior is more complex on laptops than desktops. Some recommendations (like moving RAM to dual-channel) won't apply to soldered memory. The scan will note when a recommendation isn't relevant to your specific machine type. Benchmark estimates are also wider for laptops due to higher variance in thermal performance.
